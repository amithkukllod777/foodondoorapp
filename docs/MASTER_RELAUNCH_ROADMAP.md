# 1. Executive decision

Foodondoor should be rebuilt as a hybrid commerce platform combining local restaurant and grocery ordering with pan-India packaged-product shopping. The existing brand, company history, domain and app package reduce relaunch friction, while the 45K+ lifetime download base provides credibility and a reactivation pool. The business must still prove active demand, repeat purchase and contribution margin before funding dark stores or broad category expansion.

| **RECOMMENDED POSITIONING** Foodondoor: Shopping & Delivery — one app for restaurant food, groceries, packaged products and daily essentials, with delivery speed shown honestly by product and location. |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|

# 2. Why this can work—and what can still break it

## Existing assets

- 45K+ lifetime app downloads and an established Play package identity.

- Foodondoor Private Limited, operating history and a food-delivery origin story.

- Food/FMCG sourcing, packaging, FSSAI and quality-control experience.

- Own brands including Nutriwow, creating assured initial assortment and margin.

- Local knowledge of Sehore/Bhopal and existing industry relationships.

- Kuklabs development capability for proprietary commerce, seller and operations software.

## Critical weaknesses to solve

| **Risk**                 | **Why it matters**                                                         | **Control**                                                                         |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Inactive legacy audience | Lifetime downloads are not equivalent to active buyers.                    | Measure reachable users, notification opt-ins and 30/60/90-day reactivation.        |
| Marketplace cold start   | Sellers without buyers create an empty catalogue.                          | Start curated and inventory-controlled; onboard supply against demonstrated demand. |
| Low grocery margins      | Delivery and discounts can erase margin.                                   | Minimum order, delivery thresholds, bundles, subscriptions and private-label mix.   |
| Operational sprawl       | Restaurants, local grocery and national shipping have different workflows. | Separate fulfilment modes, SLAs, cancellation rules and dashboards.                 |
| Trust and quality        | A single poor food or seller experience damages the parent brand.          | Invite-only sellers, documentation, batch/expiry controls and scorecards.           |
| Tech-first bias          | A polished app does not create demand.                                     | Release MVP early and spend against retention, not feature count.                   |

# 5. Product scope and release boundaries

## Customer app—MVP

- Mobile/email login, OTP, guest browsing and clear consent.

- Location selection, serviceability and delivery-mode visibility.

- Unified home with Restaurants, Grocery, Nutrition and Shop categories.

- Search, filters, variants, stock, offers and product detail pages.

- Separate carts where fulfilment or invoicing requires it; explain splits before payment.

- UPI/cards/net banking/wallet-supported gateway plus controlled COD.

- Address book, delivery slots, order tracking, invoices, cancellations and support.

- Ratings only for completed orders; verified product and seller reviews.

- Push notifications, WhatsApp/SMS transactional updates and preference controls.

- English and Hindi-ready content architecture.

## Defer from MVP

- 10-minute delivery guarantee

- Open seller self-registration

- National restaurant delivery

- Live-stream shopping

- Customer wallet/credit

- Complex loyalty tiers

- AI shopping assistant before search/catalogue quality is stable

- Electronics/fashion marketplace and cross-border commerce

# 8. Technology architecture

Build a modular commerce platform, not independent codebases for each channel. Start as a well-structured modular monolith with event-driven integrations; split services only when scale or team ownership requires it.

| **Layer**     | **Recommended responsibility**                                                                  |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Clients       | Android customer app first; responsive web/PWA; seller/admin web; partner Android app.          |
| Identity      | One customer identity, role-based staff/seller access, OTP controls, device/session management. |
| Commerce core | Catalogue, pricing, promotions, cart, checkout, order management, returns and settlement.       |
| Inventory     | Location stock, reservation, batches, expiry, FEFO, safety stock and reconciliation.            |
| Fulfilment    | Serviceability, routing rules, shipment orchestration, delivery slots and proof of delivery.    |
| Payments      | Gateway abstraction, COD, refunds, reconciliation, payouts and webhook idempotency.             |
| Data          | Transactional database, object storage, cache/queue, search index and analytics warehouse.      |
| Platform      | API gateway, secrets, observability, audit trail, feature flags, backups and disaster recovery. |

## Non-negotiable engineering controls

- Idempotent payment/order webhooks and duplicate-order prevention.

- Inventory reservation with expiry and release rules.

- Every price, status, refund and settlement change auditable.

- Role-based access and maker-checker approval for sensitive finance actions.

- PII encryption, least-privilege access, secrets management and log redaction.

- Automated backups with tested restore, not backup existence alone.

- Feature flags for city/category rollout and rapid rollback.

- Crash, latency, checkout-failure and fulfilment dashboards from day one.

## Core domain entities

User • Address • Seller • Merchant location • Product • SKU • Batch • Inventory location • Price • Promotion • Cart • Order • Order line • Shipment • Delivery task • Payment • Refund • Return • Invoice • Settlement • Support case • Review • Consent • Audit event

# 11. Seller and catalogue operating standard

## Seller onboarding gates

- Identity, entity, address and bank-account verification.

- GST and FSSAI validation wherever applicable.

- Brand authorisation/invoice trail and anti-counterfeit declaration.

- Signed seller agreement, fee schedule, SLA, returns and indemnity terms.

- Sample order or catalogue audit before activation.

- Category-specific document expiry reminders and suspension rules.

## Product content standard

- Canonical title: brand + product + key variant + pack size.

- Front/back/principal-display-panel images, ingredients, allergens and nutrition where applicable.

- MRP, selling price, unit/pack quantity, seller, country of origin and return eligibility.

- No unsupported health, medical, organic, natural or performance claims.

- Batch/expiry captured operationally even when not displayed as static catalogue data.

- Duplicate SKU detection and one canonical product mapped to multiple sellers where appropriate.

# 16. Testing and launch acceptance

- Functional tests for catalogue, pricing, promotions, cart, checkout, order, refund, return and settlement.

- Payment failure, duplicate webhook, timeout and reconciliation tests.

- Inventory concurrency, oversell, reservation expiry and batch/expiry tests.

- Role/access, API, privacy, secure storage, dependency and penetration tests.

- Load and performance tests at expected launch peaks plus headroom.

- Android compatibility, low-network, background/resume, deep-link and notification tests.

- Operational drills: unavailable item, restaurant rejection, courier delay, damaged parcel, COD refusal and partial refund.

- Backup restore and incident rollback rehearsal.

- Accessibility and Hindi/content expansion readiness.

## Release blocker definition

Any critical security issue, payment/reconciliation mismatch, order loss/duplication, material overselling, incorrect customer charge, inaccessible grievance path or misleading compliance disclosure blocks public release.

# 18. Go-to-market plan

## Relaunch message

Foodondoor is back—now expanded from restaurant delivery into shopping for food, groceries, nutrition and daily essentials, with delivery options based on your location and product.

## Channel sequence

1.  Legacy user reactivation: segmented push/email/SMS/WhatsApp only where consent and contact rules permit.

2.  Founder/company network and local Bhopal–Sehore partnerships.

3.  Own-brand customers and packaging QR codes.

4.  Restaurant/store co-marketing and seller-funded offers.

5.  Referral after fulfilment quality is proven.

6.  Paid performance marketing in controlled cohorts with contribution-based caps.

7.  SEO/product pages for pan-India packaged catalogue.

## Offer rules

- Prefer minimum-order and bundle offers over blanket discounts.

- Separate first-order acquisition offer from normal pricing.

- Limit coupon stacking and measure post-offer repeat.

- Use seller-funded/category-funded promotions where possible.

- Never use an offer that hides negative contribution in reporting.

# Appendix A — MVP definition of done

- A serviceable user can discover accurate products, see the correct delivery promise and complete payment.

- The order appears exactly once in admin/merchant/warehouse workflows.

- Stock, payment, invoice, shipment, refund and settlement reconcile.

- Customer receives accurate status and can reach support or cancel/return under policy.

- Operations can resolve exceptions without database edits.

- Metrics capture the full acquisition-to-fulfilment funnel.

- No release-blocking security, privacy, compliance or reliability issue remains.
