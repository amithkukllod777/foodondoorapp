# Foodondoor First 90-Day Execution Plan

## Outcome

Reach a controlled, measurable pilot—not a nationwide launch. By day 90, Foodondoor should complete reliable end-to-end orders in limited Bhopal–Sehore pincodes with exact reconciliation and no release-blocking defects.

## Days 1–15: Control and evidence

### Business and operations

- Appoint one accountable business/P&L owner.
- Confirm pilot pincodes and location-specific delivery promises.
- Approve the initial category, SKU and fulfilment-mode boundaries.
- Define pilot inventory and seller/restaurant participation rules.
- Start compliance and policy gap assessment.

### Product and engineering

- Verify Play Console, Play App Signing, update access and package ownership.
- Secure source code, backend, cloud, domain and provider access.
- Audit legacy code for security, maintainability and reusable assets.
- Extract current installs, MAU, crash data, reachable users and consent status.
- Draft current-state system and data maps.

### Growth

- Segment legacy users by geography, activity and reachable/consented status.
- Conduct interviews and intent testing; do not begin mass discounting.
- Establish baseline acquisition and retention measurements.

### Exit evidence

- Ownership/access register complete.
- Pilot geography and fulfilment promises approved.
- Legacy-code reuse decision in progress with documented risks.
- Initial economics model and user evidence available.

## Days 16–30: Product contract

### Business and operations

- Shortlist sellers, brands, restaurants, couriers and local delivery options.
- Draft warehouse, packing, QC, return and incident SOPs.
- Agree provisional commercial and settlement terms.

### Product and engineering

- Approve PRD and interactive UX prototype.
- Approve modular architecture and integration boundaries.
- Define entities, order/payment/inventory state machines and audit rules.
- Define event taxonomy, KPIs and dashboards.
- Produce release plan and test strategy.

### Growth

- Prepare waitlist and consent-compliant reactivation strategy.
- Draft relaunch narrative, app-store content and creative system.
- Define cohort and coupon guardrails.

### Exit evidence

- PRD, prototype, architecture and KPI schema approved.
- Pilot partners and operating flows identified.
- No unresolved blocker to MVP implementation.

## Days 31–60: Build and prepare

### Business and operations

- Produce accurate catalogue data and images.
- Finalise pilot agreements, support workflows and training.
- Configure stock, batch, expiry, packing and reconciliation practices.

### Product and engineering

- Implement identity, serviceability, catalogue, pricing and search.
- Implement cart, checkout, payment idempotency and order lifecycle.
- Implement inventory reservations and fulfilment orchestration.
- Build minimum admin/seller workflows and customer support timeline.
- Instrument funnel, payment, order and operational events.
- Add automated unit, integration and critical-journey tests.

### Growth

- Prepare landing pages, CRM journeys, content calendar and referral design.
- Recruit an opt-in closed-pilot cohort.

### Exit evidence

- Sandbox order works end-to-end.
- Payment and inventory controls pass integration tests.
- Catalogue and operating materials are pilot-ready.

## Days 61–75: Prove reliability

### Business and operations

- Run picking, packing, handover and cash/reconciliation drills.
- Simulate unavailable items, seller rejection, courier delay, damage, COD refusal and partial refund.
- Train support and escalation owners.

### Product and engineering

- Complete integrations, security tests, load tests and UAT.
- Verify backup restore and rollback.
- Resolve all critical and high-severity launch defects.
- Prepare release candidate and staged rollout controls.

### Growth

- Confirm closed-pilot participants and communications.
- Prepare feedback capture without substituting satisfaction for behavioural metrics.

### Exit evidence

- UAT and operational dry runs pass.
- No release-blocking security, payment, order or inventory issue remains.
- Closed-pilot release approved by accountable owners.

## Days 76–90: Closed pilot

- Launch to employees, known users and limited pincodes.
- Target 100–300 controlled orders.
- Run a daily incident and KPI review.
- Reconcile payments, orders, inventory, invoices, shipments, refunds and settlements.
- Fix critical defects immediately; use feature flags or rollback when thresholds are exceeded.
- Measure activation, search success, conversion, fulfilment, support contacts and repeat intent.

## Day-90 acceptance

- More than 90% fulfilment in the closed pilot.
- Exact payment/order reconciliation.
- No duplicate/lost order or material overselling.
- All severe incidents resolved with root-cause actions.
- Pilot contribution and repeat evidence supports—or explicitly rejects—public relaunch.
